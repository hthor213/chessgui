# Realism Verdict — Match #1, Fischer vs Kasparov

_Spec 214 realism audit · 2026-07-14 · covers `match1_fischer_kasparov.pgn` (24 games, Fischer 13-11, seed 214214) · five lens reports, each adversarially verified; corrections applied below override the original lens claims._

## Verdict

**No — not as a whole.** The match is a strong opening-book simulation wrapped around an unrealistic middlegame generator. What holds up: every one of the 24 games opens in lines both players genuinely played (zero book violations on independent replay), and the 13-11 final score is statistically compatible with the era-adjusted anchor (two-sided p 0.19-0.48 across the memo's +15..+55 Elo range). What doesn't: the compatibility is partly coincidental — both personas run the identical BT3 net at identical settings, so the generator encodes ~0 Elo delta and 24 games cannot distinguish deltas under ~120 Elo anyway — and the result texture gives the game away: 25% draws where World Championship match history runs 50-83%, a 75% White score (z=+2.28, p=0.023 vs a realistic 55% baseline), 12 of 18 decisive games decided by single-ply 2+ pawn eval cliffs, and post-2015 h-pawn storms from both "players" in five games. A casual reader of the score line would be fooled; a chess-literate reader of the games would not.

The Lens 4 numbers are freshly measured — 2,130 mover-moves scored by Stockfish 18 at fixed 250k nodes each — and they quantify what Lenses 1, 2 and 5 argued qualitatively: near-perfect background play punctuated by rare single-ply cliffs, with no move-quality gap between the two personas.

## Lens 1 — Outcome plausibility (mixed, 6.5/10)

- **The 13-11 score fits the anchor — and would fit almost anything.** With expected scores E = 1/(1+10^(-delta/400)): +15 Elo gives 12.52/24 expected, +35 gives 13.20, +55 gives 13.88. Exact trinomial P(Kasparov <= 11): 0.275 / 0.177 / 0.105; two-sided p = 0.48 / 0.34 / 0.19. Nothing approaches significance. Note the lens's 13.88/24 at +55 is correct where `STRENGTH_ANCHOR.md`'s own table (14.2, 59.3%) is wrong — that row implies ~+65 Elo and should be fixed.
- **Delta = 0 fits best of all.** Both personas use the identical BT3 net, TEMP=0.35, POLICY_TOPK=5 (`exhibition.py`); only the books differ, and most games leave book by move ~6-10. Under delta=0, expected 12.0/24, z = -0.47, p ~= 0.64. The memo's +30..+35 central estimate is never injected into the simulation; agreement with the anchor is a property of 24 games being underpowered (rejecting delta=0 at 2 sigma needs |delta| > ~120 Elo; detecting +35 needs N >= ~296 games).
- **No streakiness.** Kasparov 3.5/6 after game 6, then 7/17. Runs test on the 18 decisive games: 11 runs vs 9.89 expected, z = +0.55 — slightly *anti*-streaky. Permutation P(first-6 >= 3.5) = 0.297. (Verification: the max-over-splits swing statistic replicates only under min-segment-length 4, where p = 0.49, not the originally quoted 0.659 — conclusion unchanged. Also, the "Fischer surged" narrative this finding rebutted appears nowhere in MATCH1.md; treat it as a preemptive warning for future write-ups.)
- **Result texture is the real anomaly.** 6/24 draws (25%); White won 15 of 18 decisive games, White score 18/24 = 75% (z = +2.28 vs 55%, p = 0.023). Real comparators: Fischer-Spassky 1972 had 11/21 draws (52%); Kasparov-Karpov matches ran 62.5-83% draws (1984-85: 40/48; 1990: 17/24) — the verification *strengthened* this claim over the lens's original "60-70%".

## Lens 2 — Draw rate & termination (unrealistic, 3.0/10)

- **25% draws is roughly half the World-Championship-match floor.** K-K across 5 WC matches: 104/144 = 72% draws; F-S 1972: 52%; F-S 1992: 50%. (Correction: Fischer-Petrosian 1971 was 3/9 = 33% draws — not 56% as originally claimed — and Fischer's 1971 Taimanov/Larsen matches had 0%; the "every comparator" framing must be scoped to WC matches. It still holds there.) Even the personas' own training corpora draw at 43.4% (Fischer, 112/258) and 50.7% (Kasparov, 556/1097).
- **The agreed draw before move 60 is structurally impossible.** `exhibition.py` gates eval-draw adjudication to ply 120+ (DRAW_AFTER_PLY); the only earlier draw path is a formal rule claim (threefold/50-move/stalemate/insufficient material), auto-claimed at first opportunity. All 6 draws: 4 rule claims (games 3, 11, 14, 24 at moves 44/39/31/35) + 2 eval draws (games 1, 6 at moves 60/75). Zero draws under 30 moves; a real 24-game elite match would typically have 5-10 (K-K 1984 games 20 and 22 were agreed in 15 and 20 moves).
- **Every game ended by adjudication.** Termination tally: 15x Black resigns, 3x White resigns (|eval|>=5 for 4 plies), 4x rule claim, 2x eval draw. Zero checkmates, zero 180-ply-cap hits (max 149 plies). The PGN Termination tags literally cite the eval rule — an instant giveaway.
- **Lengths superficially plausible, shape wrong.** Match mean 44.7 moves (corrected from 44.4, which was plies/2), median 44, range 18-75, vs training corpora means 44.7 (Fischer) / 38.9 (Kasparov). But match draws average 47 moves with none short, and lost positions get demolished past human resignation points (game 5: +5.64 → mate-in-15 before the rule fired).
- **The decisive rate is driven by move quality, not just adjudication.** 16 of 24 games (corrected from 20) contain a single-move swing >= 2.0 pawns in MATCH1.md's swing table (e.g. game 2: -4.20 → +2.32; game 18: 0.00 → +5.33). With play this volatile, even a permissive draw rule would rarely fire.

## Lens 3 — Opening repertoire fidelity (realistic, 8.5/10)

- **Book integrity: zero violations.** Books independently rebuilt from `fischer.train.pgn` (258 games, 1712 EPDs) and `kasparov.train.classical.pgn` (1098 games, 6115 EPDs); full 24-game replay confirmed every booked move traces to a real game of that player.
- **First-move and defense distributions match within n=12 noise.** Fischer: 12/12 1.e4 (93% in train). Kasparov: 5x e4 / 5x d4 / 2x c4 (train 41%/45%/8%). Kasparov vs 1.e4: 11 Sicilians + 1 e5 (train 93%/5%). Fischer's Black repertoire: KID x3, Benoni x2, Najdorf, Grunfeld setup, 1...g6 vs c4 — all sourced.
- **Every "suspicious" line traces to a named real game**: 3...Bc5 Classical Ruy (Gligoric/Unzicker/Tal vs Fischer 1960-62), game 21's Exchange Ruy meeting Timman-Kasparov Hilversum 1985 ~19 plies deep, game 7's Poisoned Pawn booked for both sides from games 23 years apart, 6.h3 from Fischer-Najdorf Varna 1962, 9.Na4 Sozin from Fischer-Tal 1959, 9.h4 Saemisch via Kasparov-Spassky Niksic 1983. Fischer's 6...e5 vs 6.Be3 (game 4) is Rossolimo-Fischer USA-ch 1966 by direct move order (correction: not transposition).
- **Main weaknesses: thin-book amplification and iid repeats.** Fischer's White book at the Najdorf tabiya holds 4 games {Bc4:2, h3:1, Bg5:1}, yet Kasparov's deep Black book forced 10 Najdorfs — one 1962 game became the match "main line", sampled 5/10 (binomial p = 0.078, borderline). Games 3/5/9/17 share an identical 12-ply prefix (corrected from 11); games 11/15 and 19/23 are pairwise identical to book exit. Real match players vary and prepare between games.
- **Reporting and corpus nits.** MATCH1.md's "left book after move N" records only the first miss while EPD keying allows transposition re-entry — game 7 says "after move 4" but 21 of its first 24 plies are booked (corrected from 15). ~49/1098 (4.5%) of the Kasparov "classical" corpus are simuls/rapid and feed the book. (Corrected side-note: Kasparov's unplayed QID/QGD/Nimzo White repertoire is 158 train games, not 181; Fischer's "~4 career Najdorfs as White" is the count in the 258-game train file, not his full career.)
- Book exits at plies 3-19 (median ~11), far short of the ply-24 ceiling — confirming MATCH1's "books diverge fast" note.

## Lens 4 — Move quality / ACPL (mixed, 4.5/10)

_Method: every position of all 24 games evaluated once by Stockfish 18 at a fixed 250k nodes/move (single-threaded — contention-immune and reproducible from the PGN alone, per the memo's own recommendation #4), White-POV evals capped at ±1000cp before differencing so resignations don't dominate the averages. 2,130 mover-moves scored (Fischer 1064, Kasparov 1066), phased by ply: 1-24 book, 25-80 middlegame, 81+ endgame. Tool: `scripts/persona/acpl_report.py`; raw per-game numbers: `data/personas/match1_acpl.json`._

- **Aggregate ACPL lands in the elite band — but for the wrong reason.** Fischer mean 20.2cp, Kasparov mean 20.7cp; both sit at the top of published super-GM classical (chess.com quotes ~10-20cp for super-GMs; reported World-Championship-match figures range from single digits to ~28cp depending on engine depth, cap, and whether book moves are counted — so the *absolute* number is method-sensitive to several cp and is not, on its own, evidence of realism). Taken alone this looks human. It isn't: the mean is a near-perfect baseline plus rare disasters averaging out, not human-textured error. The shape gives it away.
- **The distribution shape is the tell: median 3cp against a ~20cp mean — a 6.7x (Fischer) / 6.9x (Kasparov) mean-to-median ratio.** Real elite players spread their loss across many small 10-30cp inaccuracies, a tighter and more continuous distribution. This generator plays *more* accurately than a human on the median move and then falls off a cliff. That bimodality — engine-tight most of the time, catastrophic occasionally — is precisely the `nodes=1` policy signature Lens 5 and MATCH1's swing table found qualitatively, now quantified.
- **Blunders are concentrated single-ply cliffs in the middlegame.** Moves losing >=100cp: Fischer 52, Kasparov 56. Moves losing >=300cp: 6 each. 90 of those 108 >=100cp blunders fall in the middlegame (Fischer 46/52, Kasparov 44/56) — the phase where book prep is exhausted and the policy plays alone (book-phase blunders: 1 and 4; endgame: 5 and 8). The twelve biggest swings are all single moves of 300-606cp and they line up with Lens 5's named collapses: G2 15...Bxd6 (Fischer, 606cp — the match's worst), G18 20...Kd8 (Fischer, 503cp), G6 57...Rxb2 (Fischer, 441cp — throwing a won ending), G15 15...Rd8 (Kasparov, 302cp — queen trapped). These are the swings that drive the 75% decisive rate; no adjudication tweak touches them.
- **Book and endgame play is engine-tight — realistic in isolation.** Book phase (plies 1-24) means 7.4 / 8.5cp with median 1-3cp: near-theory-perfect, consistent with Lens 3's zero-violation finding. Endgame means are elevated (15.1 / 19.0cp) but median 2cp on both sides — again cliff-driven, not a systematic technique deficit.
- **No move-quality differentiation between the personas.** Fischer 20.2cp vs Kasparov 20.7cp overall, near-identical per phase, near-identical blunder counts (52 vs 56 at >=100cp, 6 vs 6 at >=300cp). The two "players" are statistically indistinguishable in accuracy — a direct quantitative confirmation of the harness finding (one shared BT3 net, ~0 encoded strength delta; see Lens 1 and Harness artifact 1). Whatever separates this Fischer from this Kasparov, it is not move quality.

**Verdict: mixed.** The aggregate is plausibly elite and the book/endgame medians are genuinely engine-tight, so move quality does not collapse into obvious garbage. But the distribution shape is unmistakably non-human — a near-perfect median punctuated by rare 3-6 pawn single-ply cliffs — and the two personas are indistinguishable in accuracy. The lens fails on *texture and differentiation*, not on average magnitude. 4.5/10.

## Lens 5 — Anachronism & style (mixed, 4.5/10)

- **Book phase is the strongest part** — same conclusion as Lens 3, independently reached, including verified prefix scans (3...Bc5 = 3 real Fischer games; 1.c4 g6 = 7; Kan 4...a6 = 1 training game, corrected from 3). Game 21's Exchange Ruy reproduces the 9.Rd3 idea from Fischer-Smyslov Monte Carlo 1967 (corrected source; not Fischer-Gligoric Havana 1966).
- **AlphaZero h-pawn storms in 5 of 24 games (G4, G16, G17, G18, G22), played by both personas.** G16: 6.h4-h5-h6 in a quiet English/Grunfeld with the kingside undeveloped (and it loses). G4: 9...h5 English-Attack stopper, mainstream from ~2004+, three decades after Fischer's last game. G17: 7...h5 vs 6.h3, a post-2015 antidote. (Corrections: the count is 5, not 6; and in G22 the 6.Be2/7.Be3 setup came from Kasparov's own booked games — only 8.d5/9.h4 is policy.)
- **No persona differentiation out of book.** Same net, same temperature, same top-k — ~85% of every game is one player talking to himself. Kasparov's 8 wins (games 2, 4, 8, 10, 18, 20, 22, 23 — corrected from "six") are all harvested opponent eval-cliffs; the one initiative pawn sac in the match (G2's out-of-book 8.Qg4) is theory-adjacent rather than a persona signature. *Style claim, medium confidence — no quantitative style metric was computed.*
- **Non-human blunder texture.** Games decided by one-ply 2-5 pawn cliffs (nodes=1 policy blindness): G15, an 18-move loss in Kasparov's own pet ...b5 Najdorf via 15...Rd8?? (queen trapped); G18 collapsing in 22 moves via 20...Kd8 (0.00 → +5.33). 12 of 18 decisive games (corrected from "nearly every") have a >2-pawn biggest swing. Real Kasparov-era WC matches were ~29-38% decisive (KK85 33%, KK86 37.5%, KK90 29% — corrected from "40-55%", which *strengthens* the 75%-decisive anomaly).
- **Drift-then-blunder shape in G7 and G5.** Corrected specifics: in G7 the Fischer persona was never up material (11-11 through moves 30-64) and the eval oscillated +0.0..+1.1 through moves 30-59 — not "+2.2 decaying" — before 60...Be7 (+0.22 → +2.16) and 64...Rd6?? ended it. The near-repetitions while better and the win-by-opponent-blunder shape are real and remain the most un-Fischer passage in the match. G6's Q-vs-Q draw defense is fine technique, but only after the Fischer persona blew a -5.03 win with 57...Rxb2.
- **Sanity checks passed:** all 24 games move-legal (python-chess replay); all four rule-claim draw finals genuinely threefold-claimable at Stockfish 0.00. Some policy moves land on genuine Fischer plans by coincidence (G9's 8.g4 behind 6.h3 is the real plan of that system, though the cited Varna 1962 game itself went 6...b5 7.Nd5).

## Harness artifacts vs persona behavior

What the games reveal about the *generator*, as opposed to either persona:

1. **Zero encoded strength delta.** One shared lc0 BT3 net, identical sampling for both sides; the Fischer/Kasparov Elo difference exists only in `STRENGTH_ANCHOR.md`, never in the simulation. 13-11 is coin-flip-plus-noise around 12-12.
2. **White-score inflation.** 75% White score (15-3 decisive) is a property of same-net nodes=1 policy self-play amplifying the first-move edge, not of either persona (z = +2.28 vs 55%).
3. **One-ply blunder cliffs.** nodes=1 policy blindness produces the 2-5 pawn single-move swings that drive the 75% decisive rate. No adjudication tweak fixes this.
4. **Draw-path asymmetry** (corrected scope): threefold/50-move claims *are* honored at any ply — 4 of 6 draws were such claims before move 60 — but they auto-fire at first opportunity even when the repeating side is better, and the eval-draw path is gated to move 60+. The agreed short draw, the modal game of real WC matches, has no mechanism at all.
5. **Late resignation rule** (|eval|>=5 sustained 4 plies) lets lost positions run to demolition (mate scores appear before it triggers), and the Termination tags name the rule verbatim in the PGN.
6. **iid book sampling with fixed seed family** (Random(SEED+i), same sampler both sides): verbatim repeated opening prefixes across games, no inter-game preparation dynamics; per-game variety comes entirely from the seed offset.
7. **Post-2015 engine fashion** (h-pawn storms, engine-comfort king walks like G2's 15.Kd3) injected immediately after book exit — a net signature, anachronistic for both men.
8. **Reporting artifacts:** "Left book after move N" records first miss only (transposition re-entries hidden — G7: says move 4, booked through 21 of 24 plies); eval history appends a +/-500cp sentinel on mate and reuses the prior eval when Stockfish returns None, so the swing table is not a pure engine trace.

**Genuine emergent persona behavior**, to keep: opponent-driven repertoire skew (Fischer-as-White facing 10/12 Najdorfs because Kasparov's book dictates the defense; Kasparov never reaching his QID/QGD/Nimzo White complexes because Fischer's Black book is g6-heavy) is the realistic outcome for this pairing, not an error.

## Recommendations for match #2

Deduped across lenses, ordered by expected realism impact:

1. **Kill the one-ply blunder cliffs: replace `go nodes 1` with a small verification search** (nodes ~100-800, temperature retained), or raise nodes / cut temperature after ~move 20. This is the root cause behind the 75% decisive rate, the miniature losses, and most of the non-human texture; nothing downstream matters until it's fixed. Retune so the decisive rate lands near the historical 30-50% band.
2. **Differentiate the personas and actually inject the anchor delta.** Today the memo and the generator are disconnected. Minimum: per-persona temperature/top-k (Fischer lower T, near-argmax in endgames; Kasparov a re-rank bonus for forcing candidates). For strength: asymmetric nodes or a small eval-noise handicap, calibrated against the +30..35 Elo target with a pilot run.
3. **Add a draw model.** Allow eval-draw adjudication from ~move 30 (e.g. |eval| < 0.5 sustained 10-12 plies) plus a stochastic mutual-draw-offer in quiet near-equal simplified positions, tuned toward the 50-70% WC band. Do *not* re-add threefold/50-move claims — `exhibition.py` already honors them — but gate the auto-claim on eval (never claim when the claiming side is better) and penalize repetition in move selection when ahead by >1.0.
4. **Switch the Stockfish adjudicator from movetime 200ms to fixed nodes** (already-known fix). Time-based adjudication makes results and swing tables hardware- and load-dependent; fixed nodes makes every adjudication decision reproducible from the seed alone.
5. **Add an era prior to the post-book policy:** blend or gate BT3's top-5 against a pre-era corpus (or Maia-style human net) and penalize moves with zero period pedigree. Acceptance test: the h-pawn-storm scan that flagged G4/G16/G17/G18/G22 comes back empty.
6. **Humanize terminations:** resign at |eval| >= 3.5 sustained (immediately on a mate score); write plain "White resigns" / "Draw agreed" Termination tags and keep adjudication detail in a separate log.
7. **Scale up for any strength claim:** ~300 games (N >= 296 to detect +35 Elo at 2 sigma); keep a 24-game exhibition as a narrative sample drawn from the pool. Report White score and draw-rate/draw-length profile as standing harness health metrics; fail the run if draw rate < 40% or White score stays near 75% across seeds.
8. **Book and reporting hygiene:** filter simuls/rapid out of `kasparov.train.classical.pgn` (~49 games match `sim|rap` in Event headers); down-weight already-used branches at each game's first book branch point (kills the 4x-identical-prefix artifact, mimics preparation); report booked plies including transposition re-entries alongside first-exit; annotate match "main lines" resting on a single historical game; fix the `STRENGTH_ANCHOR.md` +55 row (14.2/59.3% implies ~+65; correct is 13.88/57.85%).

## Honest limits (spec 214: no unmeasured realism claims)

**Measured this audit:** score/streak/texture statistics against the PGN (exact trinomial, runs test, permutation tests); draw/termination tallies against PGN tags and `exhibition.py` source; full book rebuild + 24-game replay (zero violations); move-legality replay; Stockfish checks of the four rule-draw finals; training-corpus draw rates and game lengths; historical comparator records (WC matches verified against primary summaries); per-move ACPL / blunder / distribution-shape statistics for all 24 games (Stockfish 18 at fixed 250k nodes, capped ±1000cp; `match1_acpl.json`).

**Unmeasured — labeled, not claimed:**

- **Human distinguishability.** "A chess-literate reader would not be fooled" is expert judgment from the lens findings, not a blind-panel test. No such test was run.
- **Style fidelity, quantitatively.** Match-move ACPL, blunder rates and distribution shape *are* now measured (Lens 4). What remains unmeasured is a *human baseline*: the same ACPL pipeline was not run over each player's own training/eval games, so "20cp is elite-plausible" rests on published comparators rather than a per-player reference computed the same way. No sacrifice-rate or pawn-structure metrics were computed either. Lens 5's "no Kasparov initiative" claims stay qualitative (and one was partially contradicted in verification — G2's 8.Qg4).
- **Seed sensitivity.** One seed (214214) = one draw from the distribution. The 13-11, the 75% White score, and the draw count could all move materially under a different seed; the per-seed spread was not sampled.
- **Statistical power.** 24 games cannot distinguish any delta under ~120 Elo; nothing in this match validates or refutes the +15..+55 anchor.
- **Time management** is not simulated at all; no realism claim is made about clocks, time trouble, or move-time distributions.
- **Lens coverage.** All five lens reports were delivered and verified (1, 2, 3, 4, 5). Dimensions such as middlegame plan coherence and positional/pawn-structure understanding were still only touched incidentally — Lens 4 measures move *accuracy*, not plan quality.
- **The anchor itself is estimates.** `STRENGTH_ANCHOR.md` says so explicitly (no direct Fischer-Kasparov games; Regan IPR figures never obtained); this audit inherits that uncertainty.
