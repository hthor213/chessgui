# Realism Verdict — Match #2, Fischer vs Kasparov

_Spec 214 realism audit · 2026-07-15 · covers `match2_fischer_kasparov.pgn` (24 games, Kasparov 14-10, 14 draws, seed 214215, harness v2: verification search 400/560 nodes, T=0.30 top-4 by visits, jittered eval-draw gate + stochastic agreed draws, resignation at |eval|>=3.5 sustained, Stockfish adjudicator at fixed 300k nodes) · five lens reports, each adversarially verified; corrections applied below override the original lens claims._

## Verdict

**Closer — the v2 harness bought +1.5 points (5.4 → 6.9/10) and fixed exactly what it aimed at, but the match is still recognizably generated.** Every headline anomaly REALISM1 flagged is gone or reversed: the draw rate jumped from 25% to 58.3% (dead center of the 50-83% World-Championship band), the 75% White score collapsed to a healthy 41.7%, one-ply 2+ pawn blunder cliffs in healthy positions fell from 12-of-18 decisive games to 2, >=100cp errors dropped 108 → 20, resignations now land a median ~3 plies after the eval crosses -3.5 instead of running to mate scores, and — for the first time — the score points the anchor's way for a mechanical reason: the 14-10 Kasparov result (implied +58.5 Elo, two-sided p 0.43-1.00 across the +15..+55 anchor range) sits on a real injected delta (560 vs 400 search nodes), not on coincidence. But two things keep it from passing. First, the low-temperature visit sampler created a worse bot-tell than anything in match #1: games 1, 13 and 19 are identical for 34-36 plies — ten moves past the book — and Fischer loses the same game three times, so 3 of Kasparov's 7 wins are one game replayed and the effective sample is smaller than it looks. Second, the harness traded v1's "human mean via inhuman cliffs" for "inhuman mean with human blunder counts": mean ACPL 8.4-9.6cp with a 1cp median and 45-46% zero-loss moves is engine texture, not elite-human texture, the two personas remain stylistically one player (queens wholesale-traded by move ~23 in 11 of 24 games, 2 castling moves in 48 persona-sides, modern English-Attack theory from both eras' "players"), and 5 of the 8 sustained-equality draws end at exactly the ply their seeded jitter gate opens — the v1 draw pile-up relabeled, not removed. A chess-literate reader would now need the games, not just the score line, to spot the generator — that is the progress; the clone games would still give it away in minutes.

## Lens 1 — Outcome plausibility (realistic, 8.0/10) — was 6.5

- **Kasparov 14-10 (58.3%) is statistically comfortable everywhere in the anchor range.** Exact trinomial over 24 games at the observed 58.3% draw rate: two-sided p = 0.43 at +15 Elo, 0.73 at +35 (the anchor's central estimate), 0.88 at the pilot-measured +44, 1.00 at +55. The observed score implies +58.5 Elo — slightly *above* the +55 high end, but nothing approaches significance. Conditional binomial on the 10 decisive games (Kasparov 7-3): p 0.54-1.00. Contrast match #1, where the Fischer-favored 13-11 fit delta=0 best of all.
- **Plausible for (mostly) the right reason — the delta now exists in the generator.** v2 injects strength solely via search nodes (KASPAROV_NODES=560 vs FISCHER_NODES=400, nominal +34 Elo at ~70/doubling), unlike v1's shared-net ~0 delta that REALISM1 called "partly coincidental". Directionally confirmed, not statistically established: the pilot's measured +44 carries a 95% CI of [-59, +155] (includes 0), and 24 games at 58% draws can 2-sigma-detect only ~±92 Elo (better than match #1's ~120-Elo threshold, because draws shrink variance — but still ~3x the injected delta). The match is consistent with the anchor AND with delta=0 (p = 0.27).
- **White-score inflation is fixed.** White 10/24 = 41.7%, z = -1.31 vs the 55% elite baseline (match #1: 18/24 = 75%, z = +2.28, p = 0.023 — note the two z's use slightly different variance conventions; the conclusion is method-robust). The sign flipped mildly Black-ward: decisive games split White 3 – Black 7 (one-sided p = 0.036 vs an elite ~0.63 decisive-White share). 5 of Kasparov's 7 wins came as Black in Fischer's thin White Najdorf lines, so repertoire-asymmetry-plus-strength is a plausible story — monitor across seeds rather than declare an artifact.
- **No artificial streakiness.** Runs test on the 10 decisive games (KKFKKKKFKF): 6 runs vs 5.20 expected, z = +0.65 — slightly anti-streaky, same benign direction as match #1 (z = +0.55). Kasparov's cumulative score 4/6, 7.5/12, 11/18, 14/24 is a steady grind; the four-draw finish (games 21-24, two of them agreed) reads like real match-close texture.
- **The blemish: duplicate trajectories undermine the iid assumption.** Games 1/13/19 share identical 34-36-ply prefixes though the book stops at ply 24 — the sampler reproduced 10-12 identical *middlegame* plies — and all three ended 0-1 "White resigns" in the same Bc4 Najdorf collapse. Games 10/22 share 19 plies with near-identical biggest swings; 15/21 also share 19 plies (both draws); 9/17 share exactly 24 plies (the book ceiling, so not by itself evidence of out-of-book determinism). Effective N < 24, so the trinomial p-values overstate independence; collapsing the 1/13/19 triplet to one game gives 12/22 ≈ 54.5% (≈13.1/24 rescaled) — still anchor-compatible, but the headline score rests on fewer independent games than it appears. This is REALISM1 recommendation 8 (down-weight used branches), unimplemented and amplified by v2's lower out-of-book temperature (0.30/top-4 vs v1's 0.35/top-5).

## Lens 2 — Draw rate & termination (mixed, 6.5/10) — was 3.0

- **Draw rate fixed: 14/24 = 58.3%**, inside the historical WC band (K-K 5 matches 72%, F-S 1972 52%, F-S 1992 50%) vs match #1's 25%, which was roughly half the floor. Decisive rate 41.7% is near F-S 1972's 48%.
- **Count correction that overrides the circulating harness summary: the match has FIVE agreed draws (games 4, 5, 6, 23, 24), not two.** PGN Termination tags and the MATCH2.md table agree. Full tally: 7 White resigns, 3 Black resigns, 8 "Drawn (sustained equality)", 5 "Draw agreed", 1 "Draw by rule". Downstream write-ups should trust the PGN, not the "2 agreed (games 23-24)" figure in the commit/tasking notes.
- **Resignation timing is now genuinely human — the strongest lens-2 fix.** Every decisive game resigns ~1-7 plies (median ~3) after the eval first crosses ±3.5, with final evals roughly 3-5.5 pawns and no game running on into sustained mate territory (v1's game 5 ran +5.64 to mate-in-15 before its |eval|>=5 rule fired). Exact crossing plies are run-sensitive at 300k nodes — treat the per-game numbers as approximate; the structure replicates. And the model never threw away a won game: all 14 draw final positions verify near-equal at Stockfish 300k (max |eval| ≈ 48cp).
- **But the v1 move-30 pile-up survived in disguise.** 5 of the 8 sustained-equality draws fired at EXACTLY the ply their per-game jittered gate opened (g2 end 64 = gate 64, g11 69=69, g12 68=68, g21 64=64, g22 74=74; reproduced from `Random(214215+i)`'s first randint calls in `exhibition_v2.py`). Those positions were already dead-equal well before the gate and merely waited for their personal random number — draw *length* is a readout of the jitter RNG, not of the game. A naive length histogram passes; the exact-gate test fails.
- **Short draws remain structurally near-impossible.** AGREE_MIN_PLY=50 floors agreed draws at move 25; actual agreed draws landed at moves 29, 29, 35, 48, 57 and the only sub-25 draw is game 15's genuine move-18 perpetual/threefold. Real elite matches show several sub-25-move agreed draws (K-K 1984 games agreed at moves 15 and 20). Draw lengths: mean 36.8, median 35 — improved from v1's 47-with-none-short, but the short mode of real matches is still absent.
- **Draw-type mix is inverted vs reality**: 8/14 engine adjudications vs 5/14 agreed vs 1 rule claim, where real pre-Sofia matches are agreed-draw-dominated — and the "Drawn (sustained equality)" Termination tag still names the adjudication rule verbatim in the PGN (REALISM1 rec #6 half-adopted: resignations and agreed draws got plain tags, adjudications didn't).
- **The long tail is truncated.** Mean 38.7 / median 36 moves is plausible (training corpora 44.7 Fischer / 38.9 Kasparov), but max = 57 moves: no 60-90-move grinds, which real WC matches (and even match #1, max 75) contain, because the eval-draw gate plus the 10%/ply agreed-draw probability kill every long equal endgame.

## Lens 3 — Opening repertoire fidelity (realistic, 8.0/10) — was 8.5

- **Book integrity unchanged at zero violations.** Books independently rebuilt from `fischer.train.pgn` (258 games, 1712 EPDs) and `kasparov.train.classical.pgn` (1098 games, 6115 EPDs); full replay of all 24 games found no position where a legal booked move existed but a non-booked move was played inside ply 24. Every game's deepest booked move spot-checks to a named historical game, both sides (e.g. G16 to Spassky-Fischer Sveti Stefan 1992 / Kasparov-Spassky Linares 1990; G20's Alekhine to Spassky-Fischer World-ch 1972; G23 to Shirov-Kasparov Novgorod 1994).
- **The verification search made book exits cleaner, not uglier.** First-exit plies: min 4 / median 12 / max 22, one side never leaving book (G16 Fischer-Black) vs v1's min 3 / median 11 / max 19. CPL of the first out-of-book move (same 250k-node method as `match1_acpl.json`): v2 mean ~1.4-1.9cp, max well under v1's 47, zero >= 100cp (n=47) vs v1 mean 3.7cp. (Exact cp values are order-dependent — shared transposition-table state — so cite the range, not the digit.) Keep these handoff settings.
- **The regression: near-duplicate games now extend deep into the middlegame.** G13/G19 share 36 plies (shared positions to ply 50), G1/G13 and G1/G19 share 34; G10/G22 share a 19-ply prefix but are position-identical to ply 59 modulo a moves-10-12 transposition — MATCH2.md even lists the same "biggest swing" move (17...dxc5) for both. Match #1's true maximum was 17 plies (G11/G15), barely past book — the max shared prefix has roughly *doubled*, and the G1/13/19 cluster means the same game was played, and lost, three times with no between-game adaptation. Cause: T=0.30 over top-4 **by visits** ⇒ weight ∝ visits^3.33 ⇒ near-argmax once the search concentrates.
- **Color/defense distributions still match the real repertoires within n=12 noise.** Fischer White: 12/12 1.e4 (110/118 = 93% in train). Kasparov White: 6x e4 / 6x d4 / 0x c4 (train 40%/45%/8%). Kasparov vs 1.e4: 12/12 Sicilian (93% in train). One seed-luck skew: Fischer met 1.d4 with 1...d5 in 3 of 6 games vs 11% in train (binomial p ≈ 0.02) — same class of thin-sample luck as v1's 5/10 Bc4 line.
- **REALISM1's book-hygiene recommendations were not implemented, and it shows.** `build_book` is behaviorally identical to v1: no simul/rapid filter (G10/G22's deepest White booked move, 7.Nb3, sources *solely* from "Kasparov vs Ricardi, Buenos Aires sim 1997" — the ~49-game contamination flagged last time, verified 49/1098), no down-weighting of used branches (which is what allowed the G1/13/19 triple). Fischer's thin White Najdorf book still rests match "main lines" on single 1959-62 games (Bc4 = Fischer-Tal Candidates 1959; h3 = Fischer-Najdorf Varna 1962).
- **Reporting nits persist in MATCH2.md**: "Left book after move N" records first miss only (3 sides re-entered book after first exit, hidden); G16 renders the ungrammatical "after stayed in book"; booked-prefix lines silently drop opponent plies (G4: "6. Bc4 7. Bb3" with 6...e6 missing).

## Lens 4 — Move quality / ACPL (mixed, 6.5/10) — was 4.5

_Method: identical to REALISM1 Lens 4 — Stockfish 18 at fixed 250k nodes/move, single-threaded, evals capped ±1000cp; 1,847 mover-moves scored (Fischer 922, Kasparov 925), phased by ply. Raw: `data/personas/match2_acpl.json`._

- **The blunder cliffs are largely dead — the verification search worked as designed.** Moves losing >= 100cp: 20 (Fischer 11, Kasparov 9) vs match #1's 108; >= 300cp: 3 vs 12. Out-of-book blunder rate fell 6.6% → ~1.5% per persona. Only 4/24 games have a max single-ply swing >= 2.0 pawns vs 16/24 in match #1, and only two of those hit healthy positions (see Lens 5). The residual cliffs concentrate where the small search is thinnest: two of the three >= 300cp errors are one mutual game-8 endgame collapse (plies 90-94).
- **But the harness overshot into super-human accuracy.** Mean ACPL: Kasparov 8.4cp, Fischer 9.6cp — at/below the floor of the 10-25cp elite band REALISM1 used (match #1 sat at the top, ~20cp). The distribution shape got *more* extreme, not more human: mean/median ratio rose from 6.7-6.9x to 8.4-9.6x; the median move now loses 1cp (was 3); 45.4%/46.3% of moves lose exactly 0; 76% lose under 10cp; only ~6-7% (Fischer) / ~6% (Kasparov) fall in the human 30-99cp inaccuracy band. Per-game ACPL never exceeds 27cp — no bad days, no time-trouble spikes. v1 was "human mean via inhuman cliffs"; v2 is "inhuman mean with human-count blunders".
- **The personas now differ in the right direction but remain statistically indistinguishable overall.** Kasparov 8.4 vs Fischer 9.6cp (match #1: 20.7 vs 20.2 — wrong direction); Mann-Whitney overall p = 0.41, middlegame p = 0.77. The endgame bucket does separate — z = +2.67, p = 0.008, Fischer 19.9 vs Kasparov 10.2cp on n≈80 moves each — and verification found this MORE robust than the lens originally credited: excluding game 8 entirely, the separation *strengthens* (z = +2.85, p = 0.004); rank tests are not tail-driven. Partial credit stands either way: at a deliberate +34-Elo delta, a ~1.2cp overall ACPL gap that 24 games cannot resolve is roughly what a real pairing might show — but that calibration is judgment, not measurement.
- **What v1 got right is preserved.** Book-phase play stays theory-tight (5.7 / 5.4cp means, 1 blunder >= 100cp in 576 book moves vs 7.4/8.5cp and 5 blunders in match #1), and the method is fully reproducible (fixed nodes, resumable cache).
- **Measurement side-effect of the draw model:** the endgame sample collapsed from 191 moves/player to ~80 because eval-draw adjudication ends equal games around moves 30-55 — per-phase comparisons are underpowered and the overall mean is slightly draw-depressed.

## Lens 5 — Anachronism & style (mixed, 5.5/10) — was 4.5

- **The clone games are the match's biggest new bot-tell.** Games 1/13/19 (Fischer White, same Sozin-Najdorf) are identical for 34-36 plies — book ends at move 8, so ~9-10 full moves of pure search-sampled play replicated verbatim — and Fischer loses all three. No human repeats a twice-lost 17-move sequence in the same match. Games 10/22 are position-identical through ply 59. Match #1's true maximum shared prefix was 17 plies; v2 doubled it while making everything else better.
- **The era prior was never built (REALISM1 rec 5 skipped), so the anachronisms persist — but they got tamer.** The Fischer persona's main White plan in G1/13/19 is the f3+Qd2+g4 English-Attack treatment of the Bc4 Najdorf (a 1990s+ hybrid; real Fischer played f4/Qf3/O-O), the 2000s ...h5 stopper appears in G10/G22/G4, and G7 has an AlphaZero-genre 8.h4 h6. But v1's naked h4-h5-h6 storms with the kingside undeveloped are gone — the surviving anachronisms are mostly *good modern theory* rather than absurdities: similar count (~5 games), lower severity.
- **"Fischer" and "Kasparov" are still provably one player in style.** The 400/560 node asymmetry buys accuracy, never style: identical plans in the clone games, queens wholesale-traded early (both queens off by move 23 in 11 of 24 games, range moves 15-22; 17/24 games end queenless) from history's great attacker, and the match's style moments land on the wrong men — the one piece sac (G20's 9.Bxf7+) was played by *Kasparov* and lost; the poisoned-pawn grabbing (G18's 28...Qxh1, 30...Qxg2) was played by *Fischer*. The per-persona temp/topk plumbing exists in `PersonaConfig` but match #2 deliberately kept them equal.
- **Net signatures independent of the harness:** castling nearly extinct (2 castling moves in 48 persona-sides, both inside book; match #1 had 4/48 — a standing BT3 signature REALISM1 never flagged), G1's uncastled-king queenside walk (23.Kd2 ... 35.Kb3), and G6's e8=R underpromotion where a human plays e8=Q.
- **Draw and termination texture is the clearest v2 win.** The 5 agreed draws land at human-plausible moments (G5 move 29 after mass liquidation to a level R+R ending; G23 move 29 queenless and level; G6 move 57 after a long defense); G15 is a genuine forced perpetual (15.Qxe6+ Qe7 16.Qc8+ Qd8 17.Qe6+, threefold at move 18) — exactly the short sharp draw v1 could not produce; resignations mostly fire at human points (G3 after losing a whole rook, G19 down a piece). Residual tells: G9's loser plays on until Black literally promotes (48.h7 a1=Q), and the eval-draw adjudicator fires mid-combat in positions no human would agree in — G17 ends the ply after 35...Qxb2 with queens and an uncastled king in a wild Q+R+B position; G11 with queens and four rooks on; G12 mid-check-sequence with live passers. At least 3 of the 8 sustained-equality draws read as fights cut off, not games ended.
- **One-ply cliffs mostly gone:** only 2 decisive games hinge on a 2+ pawn single-ply swing from a healthy position (G8 47...Kd8 +0.16 → +3.72; G20 23.Nd2 +0.01 → -2.17) vs 12 of 18 in match #1; losses now come from multi-move drift (G1's Kc1 slide then slow squeeze), which is far more human.

## Harness artifacts vs persona behavior

What the games reveal about the *generator*, as opposed to either persona:

1. **Near-deterministic out-of-book play — the defining v2 artifact.** T=0.30 over top-4 by VISITS weights moves ∝ visits^3.33; spiky visit distributions collapse to argmax, producing 34-36-ply clone games (G1/13/19, all lost by Fischer) and a 59-ply twin pair (G10/22). Match #1's max shared prefix was 17 plies. This one mechanism simultaneously inflates Lens 1's apparent evidence (effective N < 24), regresses Lens 3, and hands Lens 5 its biggest tell.
2. **Accuracy overshoot.** 400-560 verification nodes with near-argmax sampling plays tighter than peak human classical: median CPL 1cp, 45-46% zero-loss moves. The v1 cliff signature is gone; the replacement texture is engine, not human.
3. **Strength-only differentiation.** Nodes (400/560) is the sole per-persona knob and it cannot produce style; the temp/topk plumbing exists but is deliberately equal this match. The delta itself is mechanism-verified, magnitude-unverified (pilot CI [-59, +155]).
4. **Gate-pinned draw lengths.** 5/8 sustained-equality draws end at exactly their jittered gate ply — draw length is a direct readout of `Random(SEED+i)`'s first randint, the v1 pile-up relabeled.
5. **Eval-draw rule has no tension check**, so it halts sharp Q+R middlegames (G11, G17) and mid-check sequences (G12); AGREE_MIN_PLY=50 structurally forbids the modal real-match short agreed draw; the combined gates truncate all games at 57 moves and collapse the endgame ACPL sample to n≈80.
6. **No era prior** (REALISM1 rec 5 skipped): post-1990 theory flows straight from BT3 into both personas.
7. **Book hygiene unimplemented** (REALISM1 rec 8): ~49 simul/rapid games still feed the Kasparov book (G10/G22's 7.Nb3 sources from a 1997 simul); no branch down-weighting — the direct enabler of the G1/13/19 triple.
8. **BT3 net signatures:** castling 2/48 persona-sides, early wholesale queen trades, uncastled-king walks, the G6 e8=R underpromotion.
9. **Reporting/record drift:** MATCH2.md first-miss-only book exits, the "after stayed in book" string, dropped opponent plies; the "Drawn (sustained equality)" tag citing the rule in the PGN; and the commit/tasking notes claiming 2 agreed draws where the PGN shows 5.

**Genuine emergent persona behavior, to keep:** the score now tracks a real injected mechanism; opponent-driven repertoire skew persists realistically (Fischer's thin White Najdorf book meeting Kasparov's deep Black prep produced 5 of Kasparov's 7 wins as Black — a plausible repertoire-asymmetry story, worth monitoring across seeds rather than "fixing"); and the clean book-to-middlegame handoff (first out-of-book move CPL ~1.4-1.9cp, no exit blunders) should not be traded away while fixing variety.

## What's still broken — roadmap for match #3 / spec 217 arena backends

Deduped across lenses, ordered by expected realism impact:

1. **Kill the clone games (out-of-book variety).** Two roots, both cheap: (a) down-weight already-sampled book branches at each game's first branch point (REALISM1 rec 8, still open); (b) add out-of-book variety — a temperature schedule (T≈0.6-1.0 for the first ~6-10 post-book plies, decaying to 0.30), or Dirichlet noise on the top-4 visit weights, or cross-game line memory that down-weights any 10-ply prefix already played this match. Sampling stays inside the search-approved top-4, so the anti-blunder guarantee is untouched. Acceptance test: no two games share more plies than the book ceiling (24); add a duplicate-trajectory scan (max pairwise shared prefix) to the standing harness health metrics and fail the run on violation. Note it must catch transposition twins too (G10/G22 share only a 19-ply prefix but 59 plies of positions).
2. **Fix the accuracy texture: raise sampling temperature (T 0.30 → ~0.45-0.55) and/or top-K (4 → 5-6) over visits — do not lower nodes.** Second/third search-verified choices are cliff-safe but slightly worse, lifting mean ACPL from ~9cp toward the human 12-20cp band and fattening the missing 30-99cp inaccuracy region without violating spec 214's no-injected-error rule. Acceptance test (re-run Lens 4): mean 12-20cp, mean/median ratio < ~4, blunder rate unchanged. Also compute the human baseline REALISM1 flagged: run the same ACPL pipeline over `fischer.train.pgn` / `kasparov.train.classical.pgn` so "elite-plausible" is anchored to the players' own games — this calibrates the T retune target.
3. **Give the personas style, not just strength.** Use the per-persona temp/topk plumbing plus cheap re-rank biases among near-equal-visit candidates: Kasparov — penalty on queen trades before move ~25, bonus for checks/captures/pawn advances; Fischer — near-argmax (T → 0.1) in queenless positions. Measure with queen-trade-ply and forcing-move-rate metrics per persona, not just ACPL. Do NOT chase ACPL-visible differentiation at +34 Elo — it is correctly invisible in 24 games.
4. **De-pin and humanize the draw model.** (a) Once past the gate AND sustained-equal, fire with a per-ply probability (~0.10-0.15) instead of deterministically — acceptance: <= 2 eval-draws at exact gate-open across a seed sweep. (b) Lower AGREE_MIN_PLY to ~30 plies with a slightly higher AGREE_PROB near book equality, producing the 2-4 short agreed draws a real 24-game match shows. (c) Add a tension gate: only adjudicate when queens are off OR no capture/check in the window and nothing en prise — G11/G17/G12 must play on. (d) Exempt materially imbalanced equal endgames so a few games grind past move 60. (e) Finish REALISM1 rec 6: plain "Draw agreed" tags, rule citation in a sidecar log. Standing health metrics: fail if draw rate leaves [45%, 75%], if > 40% of eval-draws fire at exact gate-open, or if zero draws end before move 25.
5. **Implement the era prior (REALISM1 rec 5, skipped twice).** Gate/blend BT3's top-K against a pre-era corpus or Maia-style human net. Acceptance: the modern-theory scan (...h5 stoppers G10/G22/G4, f3+Qd2+g4 Sozin hybrid G1/13/19, G7's 8.h4) comes back empty, castling appears in > 60% of games, and no underpromotion when queening is safe.
6. **Book and reporting hygiene.** Filter simul/rapid out of the Kasparov corpus (grep Event for `sim|rap|blitz|exhib`, ~49 games); fix MATCH2.md's book-exit reporting (re-entries, grammar, full booked prefix); reconcile the agreed-draw count (5, not 2) in the notes before anything downstream inherits it.
7. **Scale up for any strength claim.** 24 games at 58% draws detect only ~±92 Elo; the pilot CI includes 0. Run the planned ~300-game pool for the +34 nominal delta (score-based validation), keep nodes the only strength knob so the delta stays attributable, and keep the 24-game exhibition as narrative only. Track White score across seeds (+2.28σ → -1.31σ between matches); if it stays Black-leaning, look at broadening Fischer's 4-game White Najdorf book node before blaming the search.
8. **Tooling:** patch `acpl_report.py` to take `--pgn`/`--out` (this audit needed a wrapper overriding module globals); add `ucinewgame` between positions if bit-reproducible CPL is wanted (shared transposition-table state makes exact cp order-dependent).

## Honest limits (spec 214: no unmeasured realism claims)

**Measured this audit:** score/streak statistics against the PGN (exact trinomial at the observed draw rate, conditional binomial, runs tests); White-score z under both variance conventions; duplicate-prefix scans (UCI-prefix and transposition-aware EPD) across both matches; draw/termination tallies against PGN tags and `exhibition_v2.py` source, including exact reproduction of every game's jittered draw gate from the seed; full book rebuild + 24-game replay (zero violations) plus source-game spot-checks; per-move ACPL for all 24 games (Stockfish 18, fixed 250k nodes, capped ±1000cp; `match2_acpl.json`) with Mann-Whitney tests incl. a game-8-exclusion robustness check; per-ply Stockfish 300k re-eval of all 10 decisive games (resignation timing) and all 14 draw finals; castling/queen-trade/underpromotion counts by replay; threefold verification of G15. All five lens reports were adversarially verified; corrections above override original claims.

**Unmeasured — labeled, not claimed:**

- **Human distinguishability.** "Would need the games, not the score line" is expert judgment from the lens findings, not a blind-panel test. Still no such test.
- **The strength delta's magnitude.** The +34 nominal / +44 pilot delta is mechanism-verified, magnitude-unverified: the pilot CI is [-59, +155] and this match cannot resolve deltas under ~92 Elo. Nothing here validates the +15..+55 anchor beyond compatibility.
- **The 1.2cp-per-34-Elo calibration** in Lens 4 is plausible judgment; published ACPL-Elo slopes are method- and level-sensitive and would suggest a somewhat larger gap.
- **Resignation-timing specifics** (exact crossing plies, final evals) are run-sensitive at 300k nodes; the structure (1-7 ply gaps, ~3-5.5 pawn finals) replicates, the digits jitter. Likewise first-OOB-move CPL is order-dependent to ~0.5cp.
- **Seed sensitivity.** One seed (214215) = one draw from the distribution. The 14-10, the 41.7% White score, the Black-leaning decisive split, and the clone-game count could all move under a different seed; per-seed spread was not sampled.
- **Style fidelity, quantitatively.** Queen-trade and castling counts are now measured; sacrifice-rate, plan-coherence, and pawn-structure metrics still are not.
