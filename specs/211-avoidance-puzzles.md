# 211: Avoidance Puzzles — "Don't Step On the Rake"

**Status:** draft
**Depends on:** 016 (game tree), 200 (game database), 202 (annotations/eval display); enriched by the mistake-mining workstream (Phase 9 of the roadmap) but MVP does not require it

## Goal

A new puzzle type that flips the standard tactic puzzle. Chess.com/lichess puzzles all share
one shape: *"a winning resource exists — find it"* (attack, defend, or mate). That trains
pattern-finding when you've been told a pattern exists. It does not train the skill that
actually separates rating bands in real games: **sensing that the position is dangerous and
avoiding the move that loses.**

The flip (user's framing): take the position ONE MOVE BEFORE a classic "find the master
move" puzzle. You are the *other* side. Several natural-looking moves are available; one or
more of them hand the opponent that master move. Your task: **make any sound move — i.e.
don't play the move that allows the win.** It's a defending puzzle, but specific to the move
*before* the tactic exists.

This trains: threat perception without a "tactic here!" signal, prophylactic thinking, and
the real-game decision texture where most moves are fine and one is a rake.

## Puzzle Mechanics

- Board shows the pre-blunder position, side to move = the player.
- Prompt is deliberately unalarming: "Your move" (optionally "Careful…" at low difficulty).
  No "White to play and win" tell — the absence of the tell is the point.
- **Grading**: the player's move is compared against engine analysis of the position:
  - Any move keeping eval within a threshold of the best move (e.g. ≥ −0.5 vs best, and not
    crossing into lost) → **correct**. There are typically MANY correct answers.
  - Playing a trap move (allows a reply gaining ≥ 1.5 or a forced mate) → **fail**, and the
    app immediately PLAYS the punishing reply on the board and walks the refutation line —
    the teaching moment is *experiencing* the rake, with the refutation's key geometry
    highlighted (arrows on the long-range piece, the fork, etc., from 202's shape support).
  - Mediocre-but-not-losing moves → correct with a note ("safe, though 12. Re1 was more
    accurate").
- A puzzle is only valid if the trap move is *attractive*: it must look natural (see
  generation filters below). A puzzle where nobody would play the losing move trains nothing.
- Session format: mixed decks — some positions contain a rake, some are genuinely calm
  (the player must NOT learn "there's always a trap"; calm positions where any developing
  move passes keep the danger-sensing honest). Deck ratio configurable, default ~70/30.

## Generation Pipeline (three tiers, in build order)

**Tier 1 — MVP, from eval-tagged games (needs only 200 + lichess pack):**
Scan games with [%eval] for single-move eval cliffs: position P (eval within ±1.0), move M
played, eval after M ≤ −1.5 for the mover (or mate spotted). The position before M is a
candidate puzzle; M is the trap move; the opponent's actual reply (or engine best) is the
refutation. Filters: the trap must have been played by a real player (it IS attractive —
someone stepped on it), refutation must be engine-confirmed (re-verify with local Stockfish
at fixed depth, don't trust [%eval] alone), position must have ≥ 3 reasonable alternatives.

**Tier 2 — difficulty calibration by rating band (needs mined per-band data):**
The same cliff mined across the corpus: if 40% of ~1700s played the trap move but only 8%
of 2100s, the puzzle IS a 1700-level avoidance puzzle — difficulty comes from real human
miss-rates, not engine depth. This is the direct product of the Phase 9 conditional
miss-rate mining, and makes difficulty ratings honest in a way tactic-puzzle Elo isn't.

**Tier 3 — insight-conditioned decks (needs Phase 9 taxonomy):**
Decks targeted at a diagnosed weakness: "long-range piece threats", "backward-move
defenses", "quiet zwischenzug traps" — generated by filtering mined blunders on refutation
features, including *from the user's own imported games* ("positions like the ones YOU
blunder in").

## Data Model & Storage

- `puzzles` table in the app database (200's SQLite): fen, trap_uci, refutation_line,
  safe_threshold, source_game_id, themes (refutation features), band_miss_rates (JSON),
  engine_verify_depth, created_at.
- Spaced-repetition state per puzzle per user rides the same schedule store as the
  repertoire trainer (Phase 6 spec, to be written) — failed rakes come back.

## UI

- Lives in the Learn tab as "Avoidance" (live 2026-07-15) alongside future
  standard tactics.
- Reuses: board (001), shapes/arrows (202), eval bar optionally hidden until answer.
- Post-answer: full refutation replay with annotations; "why this is hard" line when Tier 2+
  data exists ("62% of 1650-1750 players stepped on this").

## Non-Goals (this spec)

- Standard find-the-tactic puzzles (separate, later — lichess CC0 puzzle dump makes those
  nearly free).
- Multiplayer/duel modes, puzzle ratings ladders.
- LLM-generated explanations (revisit after taxonomy exists; geometry highlights first).
